What are the best BMI ranges?

BMI (Body Mass Index) indicates weight status based on height. The "best" or ideal BMI for health is generally 18.5–24.9 (Normal). Here's a breakdown:

  • Under 18.5 (Underweight): May indicate malnutrition or low body weight. Aim to gain weight through balanced nutrition.
  • 18.5–24.9 (Normal): Ideal range for most adults. Maintain with healthy eating and exercise.
  • 25–29.9 (Overweight): Higher risk of health issues. Focus on gradual weight loss.
  • 30+ (Obese): Increased risk of diseases. Seek medical guidance for weight management.

Note: BMI doesn't account for muscle mass, age, or gender. For personalized advice, consult a healthcare professional.
